I came back to faith.
And felt completely lost.

I'm not someone who grew up deeply embedded in church. A few years ago I picked my faith back up — or tried to. And what I found was that coming back is a lot harder than it looks from the outside.

I couldn't find communities to join. The ones I found felt like they assumed a background I didn't have. I'd open the Bible and not know where to start — or start reading and not understand what I was reading. There was no one to ask the obvious questions without feeling embarrassed. No bible study group. No mentor. Just me and a faith I was trying to rebuild, alone.

"The loneliest part wasn't the doubt. It was not having anyone to be honest with about the doubt."

So I built SoulGuide. Not because I had it figured out — I still don't — but because I needed something that didn't exist. A companion that would meet me where I actually was. That wouldn't judge the questions. That would bring scripture to my specific moment, not a generic one. That would help me build a daily practice even when I wasn't sure what I believed yet.

The companion exists for the 11pm conversations you can't have with anyone else. The Bible reader exists because scripture shouldn't require a theology degree to access. The daily devotional exists because faith is built by showing up, and showing up is easier when something is waiting for you. The community section exists because isolation is the enemy of faith — and I want to fix that too.

If you're reading this, you probably know me. Which means you know I built this for real, because I genuinely believe it fills a gap. I'd love for you to try it. Tell me what's missing. Tell me what lands. This is better because people like you are honest with me.

With honesty,

What SoulGuide actually is

Not a church app.
Not a Bible quiz.
A companion.

SoulGuide is built around one idea: your faith journey is yours. It doesn't look like anyone else's. The doubt you're carrying, the questions you're circling, the pace you're moving at — all of it is specific to you.

Most faith apps give everyone the same content. The same devotional, the same reading plan, the same verse of the day. That's fine if you're already embedded in faith. But if you're returning after time away, or just starting out, or going through something hard — generic doesn't cut it.

SoulGuide starts by asking where you actually are. Then it builds everything around your answer. The daily devotional it writes for you. The scripture it surfaces. The way your companion listens. All of it shaped by your specific struggle and your specific journey. And it grows with you — the longer you use it, the more it holds your story.

What it gives you

Four things I wish I'd had
when I came back.

Each one addresses a real gap I experienced. None of them are gimmicks.

🕯️

A devotional written for you, today

Every morning, SoulGuide writes a short devotional using your struggle and your recent journey. Not copied from a library — written fresh. A verse that speaks to where you are. A reflection that doesn't feel generic. One practical step you can actually do today. A closing prayer that sounds like it came from your life, because it did.

💬

Someone to talk to at any hour

Your AI companion holds context across every conversation. It remembers your story. You don't re-explain yourself every time. Bring the real questions — the ones too small for a sermon, too big for a text — and get a response that actually engages with what you're carrying. No judgment. No easy answers. No pretending it's simple.

📖

Scripture that makes sense

The Bible reader gives you the full text with context. You can reflect on any passage with your companion — ask what it means, how it connects to your life, what you're supposed to do with it. No background required. No embarrassing questions. Just honest engagement with the text, at your own pace, without feeling lost.

🔥

A daily practice that actually sticks

Three tasks every morning. A soul check-in, God's message, your devotional. About five minutes. A streak to keep you accountable. A week calendar to show you how consistent you've been. Not because faith is a habit to check off, but because showing up daily is how you build something real — and SoulGuide makes showing up easier.

How it works

From first open
to daily practice.

1

Tell it where you are

A short onboarding — about 3 minutes — where you share your struggle, your goals, and a bit about your faith right now. No right answers. SoulGuide uses this to identify your spiritual archetype and shape everything that follows.

2

Open your devotional each morning

A personalised reading waits for you every day. The verse. A reflection written for your specific situation. One thing to try today. A closing prayer. Read it in the app, then continue into prayer with your companion.

3

Talk to your companion

Bring whatever's on your mind. The companion holds your story across every conversation — no resetting. Go deep or keep it brief. Read scripture together. Reflect on a verse. Or just process something you're going through at 11pm with nowhere else to put it.

4

Watch it grow with you

SoulGuide tracks your consistency. The longer you show up, the more your companion knows your journey. The deeper the devotionals get. The more specific the scripture. Faith is built by showing up — SoulGuide helps you do that.
